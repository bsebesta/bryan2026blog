The tools I use differ day by day, but they're all informed by my wrestles with ADHD, my approach to technology, and my overall rule of life. I aspire to a tech stack [like Alan Jacobs](https://buymeacoffee.com/ayjay/report-other-world).

* Curating my intentions and planning with [Sunsama](https://sunsama.com/)
* Writing in [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) or [Field Notes notebook](https://fieldnotesbrand.com/) with a Zebra pen
	* Aspirationally—typing with the [Freewrite Traveler](https://getfreewrite.com/products/freewrite-traveler)
	* Aspirationally—writing with a fountain pen
* Reading paper books or on my [Kobo Clara BW](https://us.kobobooks.com/products/kobo-clara-bw)
* Dictating thoughts with [iOS' voice memos app](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/voice-memos/id1069512134)
	* Aspirationally—a single-device digital voice recorder
* Designing with [Figma](https://www.figma.com/), [VS Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/), [iTerm](https://iterm2.com/), and sometimes with [Claude Code](https://claude.com/product/claude-code)
* Browsing the web with [Arc](https://arc.net/)
* Searching the web with [Kagi](https://kagi.com)
* Learning with [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) and whatever suits the task
* Radiowaving with a [Baofeng BF ](https://www.baofengradio.com/)
* Tracking health and fitness with an [Apple Watch](https://www.apple.com/watch/)
* Walking around with a modified / simplified [iPhone 14 Pro](https://www.apple.com/iphone/)
	* Aspirationally—a [Light Phone](https://www.thelightphone.com/)
